General Risk Assessment

Your lifestyle baseline across cancer risk

Question 1 — Tobacco

Do you use tobacco or smoke?

Tobacco is the single largest preventable cause of cancer.

Question 2 — Alcohol

How often do you drink alcohol?

Guidelines suggest no more than 1 drink/day for women, 2 for men — and less is better for cancer risk.

Question 3 — Body Weight

How would you describe your weight?

Excess body weight is the second-largest modifiable cancer risk factor, after tobacco.

Question 4 — Physical Activity

How active are you in a typical week?

Guidelines suggest 150+ minutes of moderate activity per week.

Question 5 — Diet

How would you describe your everyday diet?

Diets rich in vegetables, fruits, and whole grains — and low in processed/red meat — support lower risk.

Question 6 — Sun Protection

Do you protect your skin from the sun?

Sun and UV exposure is the main preventable cause of skin cancer.

Question 7 — Family History

Do you have a family history of cancer?

A close relative (parent, sibling, child) with cancer can raise your risk. This is not modifiable, but it's useful for your doctor.

About this guidance. CancerLess looks at the major modifiable lifestyle factors identified by the American Cancer Society — tobacco, alcohol, body weight, physical activity, diet, and sun protection — which together account for a large share of preventable cancers. This is educational guidance to support healthy change, not a diagnosis or a personal risk score. It does not replace screening or medical care.
