Breast Cancer Risk Check

A few quick questions to estimate your risk

Question 1

What's your current age?

This tool is designed for women aged 35–85 without a personal history of breast cancer.

Question 2

Which best describes your race or ethnicity?

This lets us apply the most accurate population-specific calculation for you.

Question 3

How old were you at your first menstrual period?

An earlier first period slightly raises lifetime estrogen exposure.

Question 4

How old were you when you had your first child?

Include only live births.

Question 5

How many first-degree relatives have had breast cancer?

First-degree means your mother, sisters, or daughters.

Question 6

Have you ever had a breast biopsy?

A biopsy is a procedure to remove a small sample of breast tissue for testing.

Question 7

Did any biopsy show atypical hyperplasia?

This is a specific finding your doctor would have told you about. If you're unsure, choose "No / not sure."

Before you see your guidance

This tool provides general educational information only. It is not a medical diagnosis, not a substitute for professional medical advice, and cannot tell you whether you do or do not have cancer. It does not replace regular screening or an evaluation by your doctor. Your result is based on general risk factors and does not include genetic (BRCA), breast-density, or full family-history information.

About this guidance. CancerLess uses a hybrid educational screening model built on validated, peer-reviewed tools (the NCI Breast Cancer Risk Assessment Tool and its published race- and ethnicity-specific recalibrations). No model is perfect for every individual, and this guidance is for education and motivation only — not a diagnosis. It does not include genetic (BRCA), breast-density, or extended family-history factors. Regular screening on your doctor's schedule remains important regardless of this result.
