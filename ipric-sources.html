Sources & References

The evidence behind iPRiC's prostate health information

iPRiC's educational content and AI-assisted answers are grounded in guidelines and resources from the leading authorities on prostate cancer below. This app is for education only and is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your physician about your personal health.

Clinical Practice Guidelines

American Urological Association (AUA)
Early Detection of Prostate Cancer: AUA/SUO Guideline
Primary clinical guideline on PSA screening, biopsy, and follow-up. Reviewed and updated through the AUA amendment process (2025).
Open source
U.S. Preventive Services Task Force
Screening for Prostate Cancer — Recommendation Statement
Federal screening recommendation: shared decision-making for men 55–69, against routine PSA screening at 70+.
Open source
National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN)
NCCN Guidelines: Prostate Cancer Early Detection
Evidence-based clinical guidelines used widely across U.S. cancer care (free account required).
Open source
