Sources & References

The evidence behind EDDIE's lung health information

EDDIE's educational content and AI-assisted answers are grounded in guidelines and resources from the leading authorities on lung cancer below. This app is for education only and is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult your physician about your personal health.

Clinical Practice Guidelines

U.S. Preventive Services Task Force
Screening for Lung Cancer — Recommendation Statement
Recommends annual low-dose CT screening for adults 50–80 with a 20 pack-year history who currently smoke or quit within 15 years.
Open source
National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN)
NCCN Guidelines: Lung Cancer Screening
Evidence-based clinical screening guidelines used widely across U.S. cancer care (free account required).
Open source
American Lung Association

Patient-facing screening eligibility guidance and the official lung cancer screening resources.

How EDDIE uses these sources. EDDIE's AI assistant is configured to draw its educational responses from established, authoritative medical literature like the references above. Because medical knowledge evolves, always verify time-sensitive decisions with your physician and the original source.
