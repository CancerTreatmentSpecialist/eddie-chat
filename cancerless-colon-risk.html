Colorectal Cancer Risk Check

A few quick questions about your risk factors

Question 1

What's your current age?

Routine colorectal screening is generally recommended starting at age 45.

Question 2

Has a parent, sibling, or child had colorectal cancer or advanced polyps?

A first-degree relative with colorectal cancer or advanced polyps raises your risk and may mean earlier screening.

Question 3

Do you have a history of polyps, IBD, or a hereditary syndrome?

For example: prior colon polyps, inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's or ulcerative colitis), or Lynch syndrome / FAP.

Question 4

Are you currently having any of these symptoms?

Rectal bleeding, blood in the stool, persistent change in bowel habits, or unexplained weight loss. (Symptoms have many causes — this is not a diagnosis.)

Before you see your guidance

This is educational guidance for starting a conversation with your doctor — not a diagnosis and not a screening result. Your doctor can recommend the right screening test and timing for you.
